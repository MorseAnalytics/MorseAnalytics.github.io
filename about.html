About me

Details of my professional experience can be found in my LinkedIn entry, here.

In summary, I studied at Cambridge then worked in and set up a number of healthcare-related consultancies and organisations. My specific focus has always been on data analytics, insights, modelling and software development. In 2008, I established Morse Analytics to provide greater flexibility in working with interesting people and organisations on meaningful projects.

My lifelong interest in computing and programming developed from a very young age through playing Space Invaders, Pacman and other 80’s video games in my kitchen. As a business, my father used to rent arcade machines to pubs and clubs, and there were always a few spare.

I have always sought to learn new skills, particularly in software development languages and techniques. But “good” software is as much about whether it engages the user and does a job well, as it is about being technically well structured.

I want to think I value the work I do by the positive impact it can have, hence a long-term attraction to healthcare-related engagements. I am also a strong advocate of social enterprise, the ability of organisations to take a business-like, sustainable approach to addressing social needs. I have therefore piloted an innovative approach to funding healthcare workers in low income villages in India through micro-finance, and have set up a local community cafe.

Although my familial origins are deeply embedded in Somerset, I have lived most of my adult life in Oxford. I share my home with my wife Helen, a qualified counsellor, our three teenage children, and an overly extrovert dog. Reading about nature-related topics, admiring trees, growing vegetables and playing real tennis are the main reasons I try to stay sane & fit.

Martin Morse lives in Oxford, UK.
